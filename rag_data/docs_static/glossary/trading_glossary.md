# Trading Glossary

## A

**ATR (Average True Range)**
A volatility indicator measuring the average range of price movement over a period, typically 14 bars.

**Ask Price**
The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a security.

## B

**Bid Price**
The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a security.

**Breakout**
When price moves above resistance or below support with momentum, often accompanied by increased volume.

**Bid-Ask Spread**
The difference between the bid and ask prices. Tighter spreads indicate more liquidity.

## C

**Chop/Choppy Market**
A sideways, range-bound market with no clear trend direction. Difficult to trade profitably.

**Cooldown Period**
A forced waiting period after a trade to prevent emotional/revenge trading.

**Confirmation**
Additional evidence that supports a trading signal (e.g., volume confirming a breakout).

## D

**Day Trade**
A trade that is opened and closed within the same trading session.

**Divergence**
When price and an indicator move in opposite directions, often signaling a potential reversal.

**Drawdown**
The decline from a peak in account equity to a subsequent trough.

## E

**EMA (Exponential Moving Average)**
A moving average that gives more weight to recent prices.

**Entry Point**
The price at which a trade is initiated.

**Exit Point**
The price at which a trade is closed.

## F

**FOMC**
Federal Open Market Committee - Fed meetings that can cause significant market volatility.

**Front Month**
The nearest expiration contract for a futures market. Most liquid.

## G

**Gap**
When price opens significantly higher or lower than the previous close.

**Globex**
CME's electronic trading platform for futures.

## H

**Hedge**
A position taken to offset potential losses in another position.

**High (Daily High)**
The highest price reached during a trading session.

## I

**Initial Margin**
The amount required to open a futures position.

## L

**Limit Order**
An order to buy or sell at a specified price or better.

**Liquidity**
The ease with which an asset can be bought or sold without affecting its price.

**Long Position**
A position that profits when price goes up (buying).

**Low (Daily Low)**
The lowest price reached during a trading session.

## M

**MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)**
A momentum indicator showing the relationship between two moving averages.

**Maintenance Margin**
The minimum equity required to keep a futures position open.

**Margin Call**
When account equity falls below maintenance margin, requiring additional funds.

**Market Order**
An order to buy or sell immediately at the best available price.

## O

**Opening Range**
The price range established in the first 30-60 minutes of trading.

**Overnight Position**
A position held past the market close.

## P

**PDH (Previous Day High)**
The highest price from the previous trading session. Key resistance level.

**PDL (Previous Day Low)**
The lowest price from the previous trading session. Key support level.

**Pivot Point**
A technical analysis indicator calculated as the average of PDH, PDL, and close.

**Position Size**
The number of contracts or shares in a trade.

**Price Action**
Trading based on raw price movements without indicators.

**Pullback**
A temporary reversal in the direction of the main trend.

## R

**R:R (Risk-Reward Ratio)**
The ratio of potential loss to potential gain. 1:2 means risking $1 to make $2.

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**
AI technique that retrieves relevant context before generating decisions.

**Resistance**
A price level where selling pressure tends to prevent further price increases.

**RSI (Relative Strength Index)**
A momentum oscillator measuring the speed and change of price movements.

**RTH (Regular Trading Hours)**
Standard market hours, typically 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET for US markets.

## S

**S/L or Stop Loss**
An order to close a position at a specified price to limit losses.

**Scalping**
A trading style focused on small profits from quick trades.

**Short Position**
A position that profits when price goes down (selling).

**Signal**
An indication to buy or sell generated by technical analysis.

**Slippage**
The difference between expected and actual execution price.

**Spread**
The difference between bid and ask prices, or the price difference between related contracts.

**Support**
A price level where buying pressure tends to prevent further price decreases.

**Swing Trading**
Holding positions for days to weeks to capture larger price moves.

## T

**Take Profit (T/P)**
An order to close a position at a specified profit level.

**Tick**
The minimum price movement of a trading instrument. For ES, one tick = 0.25 points = $12.50.

**Trailing Stop**
A stop loss that moves with price to lock in profits.

**Trend**
The general direction of price movement (uptrend, downtrend, or sideways).

## V

**Volatility**
The degree of price variation over time. High volatility = large price swings.

**Volume**
The number of shares or contracts traded during a period.

## W

**Whipsaw**
Rapid price reversals that trigger stop losses before moving in the original direction.

**Win Rate**
The percentage of trades that are profitable.
